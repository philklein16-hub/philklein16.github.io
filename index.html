Madrid Best: Walking + Taxis · JW Marriott is central
Metro
12 lines, 287+ stations. Runs 6am–1:30am (Fri–Sat until 2am). Nearest to JW Marriott: Banco de España (L2) or Retiro (L9).
Key Lines
L1 (light blue): Sol → Atocha → Retiro. L2 (red): Banco de España → Sol → Ópera. Buy at station machines — English language available.
Tickets
10-trip ticket €12.20 (shareable between two). Tourist Pass Zone A: 1 day €8.40 / 3 days €18.40 / 5 days €26.40.
Taxis
White with red diagonal stripe. Cross-city fare ~€8–12. Apps: Cabify, FreeNow, Uber. No need to tip but rounding up appreciated.
Toledo
AVE from Atocha. Bus C5 from Toledo station (~8 min, €1.40) or taxi (~€5–7). All Toledo sightseeing is on foot.
Granada Best: Walking + Taxis + Bus C30 for Alhambra
Alhambra Bus
Bus C30 (red minibus) — departs Plaza Nueva every 8–10 minutes. €1.60 single or €0.54 with Credibus card. Journey ~10 min. Much easier than steep walk. For 8:30am entry — take a taxi (~€7–10) to guarantee timing.
Taxis
White with green diagonal stripe. ~€5–8 across centre. Pide Taxi Granada: +34 958 28 00 00 (24hrs). Radio Taxi Genil: +34 958 132 323.
Note
Granada metro does NOT reach the historic centre or Alhambra — not useful. C30 bus is your Alhambra transport.
Seville Best: Walking · Tourist core all within 10 min on foot
Tram T1
Most useful for tourists. Runs from Plaza Nueva along Avenida de la Constitución past the Cathedral. 8 stops. Runs 6am–11:30pm. Single €1.40 — tap contactless bank card directly on the validator inside (simplest method).
Taxis
White with yellow stripe. Santa Justa → Alfonso XIII: ~€10, 12 min. City to Triana: ~€7. Apps: Cabify, FreeNow, Uber. Base fare €1.47 (day).
To Santa Justa
