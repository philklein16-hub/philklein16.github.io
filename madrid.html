Getting Around Madrid

Recommended · Walking + Taxis
Why walk
The JW Marriott is central — Prado, Reina Sofía, Royal Palace, and Retiro are all walkable (10–20 min). Use taxis for longer trips (Malasaña, Huertas after dinner).
Metro
12 lines, 287+ stations. Runs 6am–1:30am (Fri–Sat until 2am). Nearest stations to JW Marriott: Banco de España (L2) or Retiro (L9).
Tickets
• 10-trip ticket: €12.20 — shareable between two people, best value for a couple
• Single ticket: €1.50–€2.00 (Zone A)
• Tourist Pass Zone A: 1 day €8.40 / 3 days €18.40 / 5 days €26.40 — unlimited, includes airport supplement
Key lines for you
L1 (light blue): Sol → Atocha → Retiro. L2 (red): Banco de España → Sol → Ópera. Buy at station machines — English language available.
Taxis
White with red diagonal stripe. Cross-city fare ~€8–12. Apps: Cabify, FreeNow, Uber. No need to tip but rounding up is appreciated.
Airport transfer
Metro L8 + change at Nuevos Ministerios to L10 → Atocha (~45 min, €4.50–5 with airport supplement). Taxi fixed rate ~€33 to city centre. Your Blacklane arrival is pre-booked.
Toledo day trip
Renfe AVE from Atocha (33 min). At Toledo: walk uphill ~15 min or take bus C5 (€1.40, 8 min) to the cathedral area. All Toledo sightseeing is on foot.
App
Madrid Metro app (iOS/Android) for maps, journey planner, and real-time updates. Google Maps works well for bus routes.
