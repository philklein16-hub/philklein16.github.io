About the city

Toledo sits dramatically on a hill enclosed by the Tagus River, an hour south of Madrid. Once Spain's capital, it's the historic meeting place of Christian, Muslim, and Jewish cultures. The entire old city is car-free and compact — all major sites within a 10-minute walk of each other.

Heat Note

July weather in Toledo

Late July temperatures regularly exceed 35°C / 95°F. The hilltop city has minimal shade. Carry water, wear hats, plan the midday hours indoors or in tapas bars.

Afternoon

El Greco Museum & Jewish Quarter

Explore Toledo's Jewish quarter on foot — narrow alleys, ancient synagogues (Santa María la Blanca, El Tránsito), and the El Greco Museum (€3pp). The painter spent the last 37 years of his life in Toledo and his work is woven into the fabric of the city.

Getting Around Toledo

Walking + Bus C5
Station → Old Town
Toledo station sits at the base of the hill. The old city is at the top — a 15–20 min uphill walk, or take Bus C5 (~8 min, €1.40, pay driver). Taxi: ~€5–7, 5 min. On the return, same C5 goes back down.
Old town
Toledo's old city is entirely car-free and compact. The Cathedral, Alcázar, El Greco Museum, and Jewish Quarter are all within a 10-min walk of each other.
Tickets
Bus C5 single: €1.40 each way (pay driver). Tickets for the AVE Madrid–Toledo bought at Atocha Renfe machines or online at renfe.com.
App
Google Maps works well for Toledo. Download Toledo tourist map from the tourist office by the Cathedral.
